# National Defence Fund

---

## 1. Identity & Origin

The **National Defence Fund (NDF)** stands as one of India's oldest and most significant defence welfare institutions, established in 1962 following the Sino-Indian War. This government-administered fund was created to mobilize voluntary contributions for national defence efforts and ensure their effective utilization for the welfare of armed forces personnel and their dependents.

**Structured Information:**
- **Official Names:** National Defence Fund (NDF), commonly referred to as "NDF" 
- **Established:** 1962, during the aftermath of the Sino-Indian War
- **Legal Basis:** Government initiative under Prime Minister's Office administration
- **Founder/Proposer:** Government of India, Prime Minister's Office
- **Inaugural Context:** Established during national emergency following Chinese aggression in October 1962
- **Historical Context:** Created as a crisis response mechanism during the 1962 China-India War to mobilize civilian support for defence
- **Governing Body:** Executive Committee administered by Prime Minister's Office
  - **Chairperson:** Prime Minister of India (since inception)
  - **Members:** Defence Minister, Finance Minister, Home Minister
  - **Treasurer:** Finance Minister
  - **Secretary:** Joint Secretary, PMO dealing with the subject
- **Administrative Structure:** Accounts maintained with Reserve Bank of India

**Historical Timeline:**
- **1962:** Fund establishment during Sino-Indian War crisis
- **1965:** Notable contribution period during India-Pakistan War (Nizam contribution controversy)
- **2006-07:** Introduction of Prime Minister's Scholarship Scheme (PMSS)
- **2019:** Digital transformation with enhanced online platforms
- **2025:** Continued operations with modern donation infrastructure

**Sources:** Prime Minister's Office official website, Ministry of Defence records, Wikipedia historical documentation, Vision IAS archives

## 2. Purpose & Beneficiaries

The National Defence Fund serves as the primary institutional mechanism for channeling public contributions toward the welfare of India's defence community. Its broad mandate encompasses multiple welfare initiatives that directly impact the lives of service personnel and their families.

**Detailed Objectives:**
The fund's primary objective is to take charge of voluntary donations in cash and kind received for promotion of the national defence effort and to decide on their utilization. It operates with a clear focus on welfare activities that strengthen both the operational readiness and personal welfare of defence personnel.

**Beneficiary Categories:**
- **Primary Beneficiaries:** Members of the Armed Forces (Army, Navy, Air Force)
- **Extended Coverage:** Para Military Forces personnel (CRPF, BSF, CISF, ITBP, SSB, Assam Rifles, NDRF, NSG)
- **Railway Protection Force:** Personnel under Ministry of Railways
- **State Police Personnel:** Those martyred in terror/Naxal attacks (added from 2019-20)
- **Family Support:** Dependents including widows, children, and family members
- **Educational Focus:** Students pursuing technical and professional education
- **Special Groups:** SPG (Special Protection Group) personnel and their families

**Relief and Support Types:**
1. **Educational Scholarships:** Technical and post-graduation education support through PMSS
2. **Immediate Relief:** Financial assistance for families of deceased personnel
3. **Welfare Grants:** Annual funding for specialized welfare activities
4. **Resource Support:** Books and reading materials for defence services
5. **Long-term Care:** Ongoing support for dependent family members
6. **Emergency Support:** Crisis response during national emergencies and border conflicts

**Sources:** NDF official website, Prime Minister's Office documentation, Ministry of Defence circulars, PMSS guidelines

The National Defence Fund operates within a well-defined legal and governance framework that ensures transparency, accountability, and effective resource utilization. Its structure reflects the highest levels of government commitment to defence welfare.

- **Implementation Agencies:**

## 10. Beneficiaries & Usage

The National Defence Fund serves a comprehensive and diverse beneficiary ecosystem spanning across India's entire defence and security apparatus, with measurable impact on education, family welfare, and institutional strengthening over its 63-year operational history.

**Comprehensive Beneficiary Categories:**

**1. Primary Defence Personnel Coverage:**
- **Indian Armed Forces:** Army, Navy, Air Force (active service and veterans)
- **Paramilitary Forces:** CRPF, BSF, CISF, ITBP, SSB, Assam Rifles, NDRF, NSG
- **Railway Protection Force:** Specialized railway security personnel
- **State Police Forces:** Personnel martyred in terror/Naxal attacks (added 2019-20)
- **Special Protection Group (SPG):** High-security detail personnel
- **Coast Guard:** Maritime security forces

**2. Family and Dependent Coverage:**
- **Immediate Family:** Spouses, children, parents of service personnel
